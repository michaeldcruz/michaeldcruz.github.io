## The thesis

Founders come to me wanting the end state: autonomous agents shipping features overnight. My first call is always a redirect, because you can't skip to the end. I don't ask about their model or their prompts. I ask to see their deploy, their documentation, and to name the one person whose absence would break things. Those three answers tell me exactly which rung they're on and what the real first project is.

## Beats to develop

- The three questions and what each reveals. The deploy tells you how automated the paths are. The docs tell you how legible the system is. The irreplaceable person tells you where the knowledge is trapped.
- Why I don't start with the model. The model is almost never the constraint, and starting there wastes the engagement. (Link "You Don't Need a Bigger Model.")
- The sequence I walk them through. Legible, then automated paths, then agents act, then reframe the people. In order, because the order is not optional.
- The honest promise. Not "here's an agent, good luck." Walking the sequence with them so the autonomy they end up with is the kind you can trust.

## The line

You can't skip to the end. But show me your deploy, your docs, and the person you can't lose, and I can tell you exactly where you start.

## So what

If you're a founder wanting the overnight factory, answer those three questions honestly first. They'll tell you more about your readiness than any demo will, and they cost nothing to ask yourself tonight.
