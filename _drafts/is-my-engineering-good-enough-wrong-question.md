> Draft. The anxious-founder post. Take their "is my engineering good enough" and reframe the question entirely.

## The thesis

Founders ask me if their engineering is good enough. It's the wrong question because it benchmarks against yesterday's game: are we as fast, as senior, as clean as our peers. The question that predicts the future is different. How much of your system still depends on a specific person to change, ship, or understand it? Good-enough-versus-peers is a snapshot. Human-dependence is a trajectory.

## Beats to develop

- Why the comparison instinct misleads. You can be top-quartile on every traditional metric and still be structurally stuck, because every one of those metrics assumes humans stay in the loop.
- The better self-assessment: run the ladder on yourself, honestly. Most teams that think they're at level 4 are at level 2. (Link to the manifesto's ladder.)
- Three diagnostic questions to ask your own org tonight. Who is the only person who can deploy. What documentation would be a lie if we read it aloud. What would break if we let an agent open a PR on the safe eighty percent.
- The reassuring part. You don't fix this by hiring better. You fix it by making the system legible and automating the paths, in order. Anyone can start.

## The line

"Good enough" is a comparison. "How human-dependent are we" is a direction. Only one of them tells you where you'll be in three years.

## So what

Stop grading your team against the field. Start measuring how much of your company would keep moving if the field's best engineer went on vacation. Then close that gap in sequence.
