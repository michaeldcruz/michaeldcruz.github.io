> Draft. A response to every "performance per engineer doubled" chart. The chart is real. The conclusion people draw from it is wrong.

## The thesis

Yes, output per engineer is climbing. No, that is not the story. When a person's typing speed stops being the limiting factor, celebrating a faster typist misreads the whole board. The new constraints are whether an agent can read your system and whether a human can tell good from bad fast enough to approve exceptions. The 10x engineer who hoards context in their head is now a single point of failure the factory has to route around.

## Beats to develop

- The old 10x engineer's superpower was throughput and context held privately. Both are now liabilities: throughput is cheap, and private context is illegible to agents.
- The new high-leverage person externalizes their judgment. They turn taste into specs, checks, and gates a machine can execute. Their value is measured in how much they let the system do without them, not how much they personally produce.
- The uncomfortable career reframe: from doing the work to defining "good" and owning the outcome. (Reuse the closing line from the manifesto.)
- What this means for hiring and leveling. You stop paying for keystrokes and start paying for legibility and judgment.

## The line

The best engineer on an AI-native team is not the one who writes the most. It's the one the system needs the least and trusts the most.

## So what

If your top performers are still your biggest bus-factor risk, you have not built a factory. You've built a faster version of the old bottleneck.
