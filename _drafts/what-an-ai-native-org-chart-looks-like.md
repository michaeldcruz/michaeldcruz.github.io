> Draft. The org-design post. Speculative but grounded in what we've actually seen shift.

## The thesis

The AI-native company is not the same company but faster. It's a different arrangement of humans and machines. The middle layer of executors thins out, the top layer of judgment thickens, and the knowledge that used to live in people's heads moves into a shared world model the machines can read. If your org chart looks like 2020's with a chatbot bolted on, you haven't reorganized. You've redecorated.

## Beats to develop

- The old shape: layers of people executing, handoffs between them, knowledge siloed by role.
- The new shape: agents doing routine execution, humans clustered around defining "good" and owning outcomes, a world model as shared infrastructure.
- What roles grow. Taste-makers, metric-owners, the people who write and maintain the living spec, the people who design the gates.
- What roles shrink or transform, said honestly. This is the part nobody prepares for, and pretending otherwise is a disservice.

## The line

Knowledge lives in a shared world model instead of people's heads. That single change rewrites the org chart more than any tool does.

## So what

Draw your real org chart, then mark every box that exists to move information between two other boxes. Those are the ones the machine changes first. Plan for it before it happens to you.
