> Draft. The proactive-vs-reactive post. Short and punchy.

## The thesis

Most companies find out something is broken when a customer tells them. That's the reactive posture, and it's a choice disguised as a default. An AI-native system watches itself: it notices the anomaly, traces it, and often proposes the fix before anyone outside would know there was a problem. The difference isn't how good your team is at firefighting. It's whether the system's senses are pointed inward.

## Beats to develop

- The reactive loop, honestly described. User hits a wall, files a complaint, ticket routes, human investigates, hours pass. The clock started when the customer got hurt.
- The proactive loop. The system observes its own behavior, catches the deviation, and acts or escalates before the blast radius grows.
- What this requires. The same legibility that makes autonomy safe makes self-watching possible. They're the same investment.
- Agents as the watchers. Proactive instead of reactive is the last item on the AI-native list for a reason: it depends on everything before it.

## The line

The reactive company's monitoring is its customers. That's a strategy you can do better than.

## So what

Ask how you learn that something broke. If the honest answer is "a user tells us," you have a proactivity gap, and it runs on the same legibility you'd build for autonomy anyway.
