## The thesis

Every company has a few people who are load-bearing not because of what they do but because of what they know. That knowledge, trapped in a head, is the quietest and most dangerous single point of failure you have. The AI-native move is to externalize it into a shared world model, a living store the machines and the humans both read, so that knowing something and being able to leave are no longer in tension.

## Beats to develop

- The hidden liability. The deploy only one person understands, the customer history only one person remembers, the reason a system is the way it is.
- Why this got worse, not better, with more tooling. More systems, more context, more heads holding the map.
- What a world model is and isn't. Not a wiki nobody updates. A living, queryable model of how the company and system actually behave, wired into the agents that act.
- The dividend. When knowledge is shared infrastructure, onboarding, autonomy, and resilience all improve at once.

## The line

The most expensive person in your company might be the one you can't afford to lose. Fix that by moving what they know out of their head.

## So what

List the three people whose departure would hurt most, and write down what specifically they know. That list is the first entries in your world model, and the first risks to retire.
